# 4. Using Angular Signals for Client-Side State Management

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Date:** 2026-06-09
- **Deciders:** Geoff Vargo

## Context

Our app needs to manage a moderate number of client states, specifically those of: authenticated users and their
respective roles, the current user's bookings, and miscellaneous UI states. Boilerplate should be minimized for the sake
of readability and testability.

## Decision

States will be implemented and managed through Angular services which are injected as needed into components and other
classes. The states themselves will be embedded in signals and are accessed via built-in accessor/mutator methods.

## Consequences

- **Pros:** boilerplate minimized, and we avoid having to rely upon `async` pipes thanks to our use of observables.
  Stores can be easily (and cheaply) unit-tested since they're based on simple vanilla services.
- **Cons:** decreased readability because of the increased verbosity required by Angular signals. If the number of
  states to track grow too large, this approach may become untenable.
